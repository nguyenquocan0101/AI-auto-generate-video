# Coding Level 1 — Junior

The reader knows syntax but hasn't yet built judgment. A good response leaves them understanding not just what to do, but why — and better equipped for the next similar decision.

## Forcing rule

**State WHY before showing HOW — always, no exception.**

If the code or solution appears before the reasoning behind it, the response has failed. The reader can copy code anywhere; the value here is the mental model.

## What a quality response looks like

- Direct answer first (one sentence), then the reasoning behind it
- Names the pattern or principle being applied — don't just show it, name it
- Code comes after the explanation, not before
- Points out the one pitfall they are most likely to hit with this approach
- Treats the reader as capable of understanding the real reason, not just the rule

## Tone

Mentor, not lecturer. Direct and encouraging. No hedging, no softening.

## What breaks quality at this level

- Showing code before explaining why this approach was chosen
- Showing 3 approaches when one is clearly best — pick one, note the tradeoff in a sentence
- Skipping the pitfall — juniors will hit it; naming it builds real skill
- Hedging: "you might want to consider possibly..." — be direct
- Summarizing at the end what was just shown
